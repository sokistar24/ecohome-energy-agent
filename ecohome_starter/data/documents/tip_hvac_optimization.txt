Heating and cooling are usually the largest part of a home's energy use, so optimising your HVAC system delivers the biggest savings. The goal is to heat or cool only when needed, only the spaces you use, and to shift demand away from expensive peak pricing hours where possible. Most modern systems support scheduling, zoning, and smart controls that make this straightforward.

Thermostat Settings:
- Set heating to around 18-20°C in winter and cooling to around 24-26°C in summer for a good balance of comfort and savings
- Lower the heating by 1°C to cut heating energy use by roughly 8-10% over a season
- Set back the temperature by 3-5°C overnight or when the house is empty
- Avoid setting an extreme temperature to "heat up faster"; the system heats at the same rate regardless of the target

Smart and Programmable Thermostats:
- Use a programmable schedule so the system eases off while you sleep or are at work
- Smart thermostats learn your routine and pre-heat or pre-cool before you arrive
- Pre-cool or pre-heat the home during cheap off-peak hours, then coast through expensive peak periods
- Use geofencing so the system adjusts automatically when the last person leaves

Zoning and Targeted Heating:
- Heat or cool only the rooms you are using rather than the whole house
- Close vents or radiator valves in unused rooms and keep their doors shut
- Use multi-zone controls or smart radiator valves to set different temperatures per room
- Consider a portable heater for a single occupied room instead of running central heating

Heat Pumps and Efficient Equipment:
- Air-source heat pumps deliver 3-4 units of heat per unit of electricity, far more efficient than direct electric heating
- Run a heat pump steadily at a lower flow temperature rather than in short hot bursts
- Size any replacement system correctly; an oversized unit cycles on and off and wastes energy
- Look for high-efficiency ENERGY STAR or equivalent rated equipment when replacing old units

System Maintenance:
- Change or clean air filters every one to three months to maintain airflow and efficiency
- Bleed radiators annually so they heat evenly without cold spots
- Clean outdoor heat pump and air-conditioner coils once a year
- Have the system serviced annually to catch faults before they waste energy

Reducing the Load:
- Seal draughts around windows, doors, and loft hatches so heated air does not escape
- Insulate the loft, walls, and hot water pipes to reduce how hard the system must work
- Use curtains and blinds to keep heat in during winter and block solar gain in summer
- Let in winter sun during the day and close curtains at dusk to retain warmth
