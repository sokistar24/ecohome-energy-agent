Energy needs change with the seasons, so the smartest savings strategy changes too. Winter is dominated by heating, summer by cooling and high solar generation, and the milder shoulder seasons offer a chance to use very little energy at all. Adjusting your habits and automations for each season keeps bills low all year round.

Winter Heating Strategy:
- Set heating to a steady 18-20°C and wear warmer layers rather than overheating the house
- Let in sunlight during the day and close curtains at dusk to trap warmth
- Pre-heat the home during cheap off-peak hours and reduce heating during expensive evening peaks
- Service the heating system before winter and bleed radiators so they run efficiently

Summer Cooling Strategy:
- Set cooling no lower than 24-26°C and use fans, which use far less energy than air conditioning
- Close blinds and curtains on sunny sides of the house to block solar heat gain
- Ventilate at night and early morning when outside air is cool, then shut up the house by day
- Run cooling during the solar generation window so panels offset the load

Spring and Autumn Opportunities:
- Use natural ventilation instead of heating or cooling whenever the weather allows
- These shoulder months are ideal for tackling maintenance like servicing and insulation
- Take advantage of mild days to air-dry laundry outdoors instead of using the tumble dryer
- Re-check and update your automation schedules as daylight hours shift

Seasonal Solar Patterns:
- Expect much higher solar generation in summer and plan to self-consume more then
- In winter, lean more on cheap off-peak grid hours since solar output is limited
- Shift the timing of your big loads earlier or later in the day as the solar window changes
- Use seasonal generation data to set realistic expectations for each part of the year

Seasonal Water Heating:
- Lower hot water temperature slightly in summer when incoming water is warmer
- Insulate the hot water tank and pipes to reduce standby losses, especially in winter
- Use solar or off-peak power to heat water rather than expensive peak electricity
- Heat water once during the cheapest window rather than reheating throughout the day

Year-Round Habits:
- Review your energy bills each season to spot rising usage early
- Adjust thermostat schedules at the start of each season rather than leaving them fixed
- Keep filters, vents, and panels clean as part of a seasonal maintenance routine
- Plan major appliance use around the cheapest energy source available in that season
