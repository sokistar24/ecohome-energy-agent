Energy storage, mainly home batteries, lets you decouple when you generate or buy energy from when you use it. The core idea is simple: store energy when it is cheap or free, and use it when it would otherwise be expensive. A well-managed battery shaves the costly evening peak off your bill and squeezes more value from rooftop solar.

How Battery Storage Saves Money:
- Store cheap off-peak or surplus solar energy and use it during expensive peak hours
- Avoid buying grid electricity at the highest rates by discharging the battery then
- Reduce or eliminate exporting solar cheaply by keeping that energy for your own evening use
- Flatten your demand so you draw less power during peak periods that carry demand charges

Charging Strategy:
- Charge primarily from surplus solar during the day when generation exceeds your usage
- Top up from cheap off-peak grid power overnight when solar alone is not enough
- On an agile or time-of-use tariff, charge automatically when the half-hourly price is lowest
- Avoid charging during peak price periods unless absolutely necessary

Discharging Strategy:
- Discharge the battery during the expensive evening peak to cover your highest-cost hours
- Prioritise using stored energy for high-power evening loads like cooking and heating
- Hold some reserve if you want backup power during an outage
- Let the battery recharge from solar or off-peak power once the peak has passed

Sizing and Selecting a Battery:
- Size the battery to cover your typical evening and peak-period usage, not your whole day
- An oversized battery costs more upfront and may rarely use its full capacity
- Consider usable capacity and round-trip efficiency, since some energy is lost in storage
- Match the battery's power rating to the appliances you want it to run at once

Working with Solar and Tariffs:
- Solar-plus-storage lets you self-consume far more of your generation than solar alone
- Charge from solar first and only fall back to grid power when generation is insufficient
- Combine the battery with a time-varying tariff to arbitrage cheap and expensive hours
- Track how much of your peak usage the battery offsets and tune the schedule over time

Battery Health and Longevity:
- Avoid routinely draining the battery completely; partial cycles are gentler on it
- Keep the battery within its recommended temperature range for best lifespan
- Use the manufacturer's app to monitor performance and state of health
- Review and update charge and discharge schedules as your usage and tariff change
